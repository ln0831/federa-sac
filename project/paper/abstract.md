# Abstract Draft

Status:
- evidence-backed draft for the current empirical-analysis route
- revised after the corrected multi-seed ablation, the clean main rerun, and the fresh Q1 main replica

This paper studies how to evaluate topology-aware federated control methods for active voltage control under topology shift on the case141 benchmark. Instead of relying on mixed-context summaries, we use a deterministic, context-aligned runtime bundle that pairs each federated method with matched baseline rollouts and reports paired seed deltas for return, voltage violation, and active-power loss. The current evidence is mixed in a revealing way. In the historical three-seed main suite, `fedgrid_topo_proto` and `fedgrid_v4_cluster_distill` are both negative on the `random_reset` benchmark, with mean paired DeltaReturn `-0.106` and `-0.173`, respectively. In a clean current-workspace three-seed rerun, `fedgrid_topo_proto` becomes weakly positive at `+0.122` while `fedgrid_v4_cluster_distill` remains negative at `-0.055`. However, in a fresh independent three-seed main replica, `fedgrid_topo_proto` returns to slightly negative paired DeltaReturn at `-0.019` while `fedgrid_v4_cluster_distill` is clearly negative at `-0.187`. A corrected three-seed ablation further shows that `fedgrid_topo_proto` is the only positive ablation result, while `fedgrid_v4_cluster_distill`, `fedgrid_v4_cluster_nodistill`, and `fedgrid_v4_cluster_gentle` are all negative on paired return. The current evidence therefore supports a paper about evaluation discipline, reproducibility, and mechanism-specific sensitivity rather than a broad clustered-method superiority claim. We release the runnable training, deterministic evaluation, aggregation, table, figure, and reporting pipeline together with the current suite artifacts so the conclusions remain traceable and reproducible.
